Autumn running in London

The humid heat of summer is long gone, but the chilling winds of winter are yet to arrive. As the leaves begin to turn and temperatures stay mind, autumn is the best time for running in the British captial.

With its abundant parks and leafy riversice paths, London really is a runner's city. These fantastic autumn races allow you to combine sightseeing with your exercise regime; and best of all, there's a medal at the end. Our Park Inn by Radisson Hotel and Conference Centre London Heathrow markes the ideal base for a running weekend in London, with plenty of comforts for recovery (including a jacuzzi) and frequent pulic tranpost links into the city centre

River Thames Half Marathon

This scenic half marathon takes you 13.1 miles along the banks of the River Thames, on the road from Walton town center to the Xcel Leisure Centre. Most of the router after that follows the river townpath, although there are a few miles of road work. Look out for Hampton Court Bridge around the halfwway mark. The race route is very flat, so it you don't like running up hills or want to set a new personal best, this is perfect for you. It also makes the router particularly suitable for less exprerienced races, so it's a good choice for you first half marathon. registration stays open until 16 October, and every finisher gets to walk home with a goodle bag and a bespoke madal.

When: 18 October

Where: Walton-on-Thames

Entry Fee: £29 (£27 width a UKA competitions Licence)
